Our Mission

"To fight America’s obesity epidemic by providing K-12 students nutritious and tasty meals by efficiently connecting schools, families, and local restaurants through our website."

Our Story

NomNom Foods was founded by two Harvard students who are driven by their education, experiences in the social sector, and training in morality and ethics; inspired by the opportunities enabled by technology, and ready to fight obesity in America. NomNom Foods believes that eating nutritious meals at the most impressionable period of a person’s life helps build a foundation for happiness and success.

Our Team

Lauren Swersky

Lauren began her career as a Teach for America corps member in New York City, where she saw firsthand the poor quality of school food and its effects on student achievement. During her tenure working with the Ministry of Education of Spain, she was inspired by schools’ emphasis on health education and ability to provide all students with nutritious lunches. She studied these topics, in addition to education entrepreneurship and policy, in depth at Harvard Graduate School of Education, where she received a Masters of Education. When she’s not working, you can find her cooking, reading, or creating amateur documentaries.

Sunha Kim

Sunha is a graduate of The Ohio State University and holds a Masters of Theological Studies from Harvard Divinity School. His experience primarily lies in political grassroots organizing, having taken substantial roles in major political campaigns. Furthermore, he has been passionate about social entrepreneurship for a decade. Sunha’s vision for NomNom Foods to provide healthy meals to students lies within his broader training in morality, ethics, and social justice that he received while at Harvard.
